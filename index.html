"JRD TATA"

[ Words : ] Always aim at perfection for only then will you achieve excellence.

[1904 - 1993]

One of the most brilliant characters, creating his own passion with powerful thoughts that enlightened the new age, came in the world with the aim to built and establish his extraordinary personality. This is a short write-up on the lifestyle of legend none other than the adventurous aviator, industrialist, entrepreneur and the past chairman of Tata company - Jehangir Ratanji Dadabhoy Tata.

JRD Tata, a man with the multifaceted personality was born to Sooni (French women) and RD Tata (Indian businessman) in 29 July 1904 in Paris, France. Because of his parents, he was successful in inculcating right values, moral ethics and spirituality in his soul.

He saw Louis Bleriot (an early Pioneer in an aviation who lived near to his house) flying his early planes gave him an inspirational lifelong passion for planes, alive dreams that he would breathe in every single moment.

He pursued his studies from France, Japan and England and then served in France's army for years. Then when he had turned up to a teen in 1925 was sent to Jamshedpur for apprenticeship.

Next to his mother's death because of tuberculosis, his father also left him, which made him feel very lonely. Hard to overcome but again there stand he with unbreakable, unbeatable confident JRD. With his continuous determination and hard work Tata took up the new responsibility to play the role of director in Tata company. He surrendered his French citizenship in 1929. Crossing so many hurdles, he finally succeeded in acquiring his Pilot license and became the first Indian to fly the first mail service in India.

At the age of 34 his reputation clicked up to another level as he was selected as the chairman of TATA company (company that was established in 1868 by Tata's great grandfather - Jamsetji Nusserwanji Tata).

He had great concern for the dignity of his employees. Politeness, kindness and the way he served to the society increased deep respect and love in the heart of many peoples. He influenced many peoples from his tremendous positive action.

In 1932, he established an air mail courier service, and later in 1953 was nationalized and transformed from Tata Airlines to Air India. Businesses of steel, parks and hotels expanded over the years.

The pride of Tata company felt more proud in the prestigious moment when JRD became jewels of India by receiving many prestigious awards, some of them are Bharat Ratna (in 1992) and Padma vibhushan (in 1957). In 1991, he retired at the age of 87.

Millions of hearts became sad, hundreds of crores eye teared up when they came to know that Tata was no more. He was suffering from kidney infection. On 29 November 1993, around 5:30 a.m. at Geneva hospital, Switzerland. Tata took his last peaceful breath. On his death, the Indian parliament in an unusual gesture for private citizens, was adjourned in his memory.
